## Analysing the Bank Stock

### Overview

**South Indian** Bank's share price has remained stagnant despite being an undervalued stock 
with a **low Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratio of 5.94** compared to the industry 
average of 15-20. The video breaks down the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
 to identify factors hindering its growth:

**Return on Assets (ROA):** NetIncome/AVG Total Assets Expectation: ROA > 1% (Base) 

Banks Total Assets are Interest from the Loans, Investments, Fixed Assets and Regulatorary Deposits(SLR and CLR).

---

**Net Interest Margin (NIM):** Higher Interest Loan, and lower interest on deposit, and revenue from other services
apart from the Loan Interest like Non Interest Income services like High mortage housing loan, vehicle, Insurance and creditCard.

**Non-Performing Assets (NPA):** Loss from the non recoverable loans and assets. Lesser the value is Good

**Cost-to-Income Ratio:** The bank manages its operational expenses to generate profit.
Revenue Sources: A major point of concern is the bank's heavy reliance on traditional interest income, lacking a significant contribution from non-interest income sources like mutual funds, insurance, and credit cards
